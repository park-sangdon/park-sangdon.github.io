AxGS Lab centers on AI x Games Systems and prioritizes building real systems, services, and games with AI-assisted development. The goal is to optimize model quality, reliability, cost, and deployment speed within one operational frame.

Academic and Industry Path

I received my BS and MS in Mathematical Sciences from KAIST, and my PhD from the School of Electrical Engineering at KAIST. I worked at KAIST's Institute of IT Convergence from 2017 to 2025, then moved to SayBerry Games before joining Daejeon University.

Current Direction

The research agenda is organized around AI x Games Systems, trustworthy AI, and optimization-driven decision making, with a strong emphasis on reproducible code and deployable outputs.

Research Areas

What the lab works on

The categories below match the reference site structure, but they are written as execution-oriented research themes.

LLM System Design

Designing RAG, tool use, and agent orchestration from requirements definition to deployment pipelines.

Trustworthy AI

Quantifying hallucination, uncertainty, and policy risks with explicit test benches and operational baselines.

Optimization & Game Theory

Turning pricing, resource allocation, and scheduling problems into implementable mathematical policies.

Edge / IoT

Analyzing latency, cost, and quality tradeoffs under resource-constrained environments.

AI-Driven Development

Building workflows that convert research code into operating code through experimentation and release discipline.

Applications

Evaluating game, conversational, and educational systems with user-facing metrics rather than offline-only benchmarks.
